## Derived Variable Notes

- `age_at_index` is calculated from birth date and encounter start.
- `length_of_stay_days` is calculated from encounter stop minus encounter start.
- `readmitted_30d` is derived from the next inpatient encounter after discharge.
- `outpatient_followup_7d`, `outpatient_followup_14d`, and `outpatient_followup_30d` are derived from outpatient or ambulatory encounters after discharge and within the specified timing window.
- `days_to_first_outpatient_followup` is calculated from discharge to the first qualifying outpatient or ambulatory encounter after discharge.
- ED-only and observation-only revisits may be summarized separately but are not the primary inpatient readmission outcome.
- Comorbidity flags are derived from condition records.
- Literature-informed optional variables such as post-acute setting, functional status proxies, discharge medication count, and discharge-adjacent labs will only be used if the Synthea export supports defensible derivation.
- Final variable availability may depend on the Synthea export format.

## Outpatient Follow-Up Notes

Balasubramanian et al. (2025) motivates examining outpatient follow-up in relation to 30-day readmission using clearly defined timing windows, including 7-day, 14-day, and 30-day periods. The paper also reinforces that follow-up, readmission, ED revisit, and mortality are distinct post-discharge outcomes that require separate definitions.

This project adapts those ideas for a synthetic Synthea portfolio workflow. Follow-up variables will be used as descriptive and exploratory analytic measures only. Cases where readmission occurs before outpatient follow-up will be flagged because temporal sequencing can bias interpretation in retrospective observational analyses. Subgroup and risk stratification variables, such as age group, chronic condition count, and prior utilization, will be included when the synthetic export supports them.
